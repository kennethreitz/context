1. {{Puritan Great Migration}} == Biography == Philemon Whale was a weaver.<ref name=Hunt>John G. Hunt, "Ancestry ofPhilemon Whale," in 'The New England Historical and Genealogical Register, Vol 114 (Boston, MA: New England Historic Genealogical Society, 1960): 155, citing Parish Registers of Chickney, Essex, England: "Baptized: 1599--27 May, Philemon, son of Philemon Whale." [http://www.americanancestors.org/databases/new-england-historical-and-genealogical-register/image/?pageName=155&volumeId=11737&filterQuery=databasename:New%20England%20Historical link]</ref> He was christened 27 May 1599 in Chickney, Essex, England.<ref name=Hunt/> He was the son of Cleric Philmon Wale of Chickney and possibly his wife Agnes or Ann Norwood.<ref name=Hunt/> He was named along with his siblings Jonas, Henry, Mary andElizabeth in the will of his uncle John Whale dated March 21, 1609 proved May 4 1609.<ref>"The will of John Whale nowe in the parish of St. Mary, Towne of Colchester, County of Essex" in The New England Historical and Genealogical Register, Vol 63 (Boston, MA: New England Historic Genealogical Society): 35 [http://www.americanancestors.org/databases/new-england-historical-and-genealogical-register/image/?pageName=35&volumeId=11606&rId=242354918 link]</ref><ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> His father's will dated 4 Apr 1618 probated 19 May 1620 names his wifeAgnes and two sons: Jonas and Philemon.<ref name=Hunt/> He was made afreeman on 10 May 1648 (C. R., Vol. II. p. 202) and purchased land inSudbury before 1643.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> Agnes named in his father's will, relict of Philemon Whale, cleric, may have been Anne Norwood who Philemon Whale married 4 Sept. 1587, according to the parish registers of Great Canfield, Essex.('NEHGR 114:155 citing Col. Banks’MSS in the Library Congress; The Register, vol 53, p. 303).<ref name=Hunt/> Philemon Whale has been the subject of exhaustive research. Unfortunately older erroneous legacy publications have been the source of confusion. However, his story helps us elucidate and identify many early connections in Sudbury. We know now he married three times.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale>Mary Walton Ferris, "Whale" in Dawes-Gates Ancestral Lines, A memorial volume: The American ancestry of Rufus R.Dawes, Vol 1 (Wisconsin: Cuneo Press, 1931-43): 660 -- 665 [http://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/005730222 link]</ref><ref name=DawesGatesWright>Mary Walton, "Wright" in Ferris, Dawes-Gates Ancestral Lines, A memorial volume: The American ancestry of RufusR. Dawes, Vol 1 (Wisconsin: Cuneo Press, 1931-43) p 678 -- 686 [http://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/005730222 link]</ref><ref name=DawesGatesMoore>Mary Walton Ferris, "Moore" in Dawes-Gates Ancestral Lines, A memorial volume: The American ancestry of Rufus R.Dawes, Vol 1 (Wisconsin: Cuneo Press, 1931-43) p 446 - 450 [http://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/005730222 link]</ref><ref name=TAG66>John Plummer, "The English Origin of John1 Moore of Sudbury, Massachusetts," The American Genealogist Vol 66 (New Haven, Conn.: D.L. Jacobus, Jan 1991): 75-77 [http://www.americanancestors.org/databases/american-genealogist-the/image/?pageName=75&volumeId=12961&filterQuery=BROWSE link]</ref><ref name=TG6>Robert Charles Anderson, ""The English Origins of Philemon Whale of Sudbury, Massachusetts," The Genealogist Vol 6 (1985): 131-4</ref> He married first to Elizabeth (Frost) Rice the daughter of Edward Front and Thomasine Belgrave on 24 Jan 1621 in Chickney, Essex,England.<ref name=TAG66/> She was the widow of Henry Rice of Subury.<ref name=TAG66/> <ref name=DawesGatesWhale/><ref name=DawesGatesMoore/> Philemon Whale adopted Elizabeth Rice thebiological of daughter of Elizabeth (Front) Rice and the late Henry Rice. <ref name=TAG66/><ref name=TG6/> Philemon' stepdaughter, Elizabeth Rice, changed her name to Elizabeth Whale before she married to John Moore of Sudbury. <ref name=TAG66/><ref name=TG6/> (Note previous genealogies had assumed Elizabeth Whale was Philemon Whale's biological daughter). After his first wife died in 20 JUN 1647 in Sudbury, Philemon Whale married second to Sara (unknown) the widow of Thomas Cakebread on 7 Nov1649 in in Sudbury.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> She was the mother of Mary Cakebred who married Ensign John Grout.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> Mary Cakebred and Ensign John Grout had a son John Grout who marriedRebecca Toll. After the death of Thomas Cakebread Ensign John Grout is recorded as being in possession of eight parcels of land in Watertown, seven of which had been owned previously by Thomas Cakebread and after John Grout moved to Sudbury he took over the operation of the millthat Thomas Cakebread had built.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> In 1670 court records Ensign John Grout called the late Thomas Cakebread his father-in-law. These items together prove Ensign John Grout's first wifeMary was the daughter of Thomas and Sara Cakebread.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> Sara died on 28 DEC 1656 in Sudbury and Philemon Whale married a third and final time to Elizabeth (Uknown) Griffen on 9 Nov 1657 in Sudbury ("WHALE, Philemon and Elizabeth Griftine, Nov. 9, 1657. MR Subury marriages p 282)").<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/><ref name=Upton>Mary Walton, "Upton" in Ferris, Dawes-Gates AncestralLines, A memorial volume: The American ancestry of Rufus R. Dawes, Vol 1 (Wisconsin: Cuneo Press, 1931-43) p 639 -- 641 [http://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/005730222 link]</ref><ref name=SudburyMR>Sudbury, Middlesex, Massachusetts, United States.Vital Records of Sudbury, Massachusetts, to the Year 1850. (Boston, Massachusetts: New England Historic Genealogical Society, 1903)</ref> Elizabeth (Uknown) Griffen had married first to an unknown man named Upson with whom she had a daughter Hannah Upson (1638 - 1708) who married Edward Wright (1634-1703) on 18 Jun 1659 in Sudbury. <ref name=SudburyMR/><ref name=Upton/> Many unsuccessful attempts have been made to identify her first husband. As widow Elizabeth (Unknown) Upson she married second to Hugh Griffin of Sudbury on before Feb 1637 and they had four more children: Abigail, Sarah, Shemuel and Jonathan.<ref name=SudburyMR/> He died on 21 Jun 1656 or 27 Jun 1656<ref>"Sudbury Records," The New England Historical and Genealogical Register Vol 17 (Feb 1863): 254</ref><ref name=SudburyMR/> (Researchers should take care not to conflate this couple of Sudbury and their children with Humphrey Griffin who married Elizabeth Andrews in Ipswich whose will was probated in 19 Nov 1661). Elizabeth (Unknown) Griffin married a third time to Philemon Whale after the death of Hugh Griffin as Philemon's final wife. <ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> <ref name=Upton/> <ref name=SudburyMR/> He died in Sudbury 24 Feb 1675.<ref name=Sudbury /> Philemon Whale's will was signed on January 19, 1675 in Sudbury and an inventory of hisestate was taken on March 28, 1676, showing a valuation of over £87.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> He left his entire estate to his third wife Elizabeth and after her death "unto ye foure sonnes of my loveing daughter ELIZABETH Moores, vizt. William, Jacob, Joseph and BENJAMIN equally to be divided amongst them." (Dawes-Gates citing PCC wills, reg. Soames, 1620)<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> He was clearly referring to his adopted daughter Elizabeth Rice (1612-1690) who was now the widow of John Moore (1602-1673) and also to their four sons.<ref name=DawesGatesWhale/><ref name=DawesGatesWhale/> These probate records make it clear that Philemon Whale, Weaver of Sudbury had no biological children of his own and no record of such has been found. His third wife survived him and two months after Philemon Whale's death on 21 Apr 1676, Sudbury was attached by 500 - 1,000 of King Phillip's soldiers.
